Leonardo da Vinci

Renaissance painter, sculptor, architect, inventor, military engineer and draftsman.

Here is a timeline of Leonardo da Vinci's life:

  • 1452: Born in Anchinao, Italy
  • 1467: Becomes An Apprentice To Andrea Del Verrocchio, In Florence.
  • 1472: Accepted into the painters' guild of Florence.
  • 1476: Accused of sodomy (charges were later dropped).
  • 1478: The Annunciation is painted.
  • 1481: Begins to work on The Adoration of the Magi.
  • 1482: Moves to Milan to work in the service of the city's duke, Ludovico Sforza.
  • 1483: Paints Virgin of the Rocks.
  • 1485: Paints Lady with an Ermine.
  • 1495: Begins to work on The Last Supper.
  • 1498: Completes The Last Supper.
  • 1499: Leaves Milan and spends time in Venice.
  • 1500: Begins painting the Virgin and Child with Saint Anne.
  • 1500: Returns to Florence.
  • 1502: Begins work as senior military architect and general engineer for Cesare Borgia, son of Pope Alexander VI.
  • 1503: Commissioned to paint the Mona Lisa.
  • 1503: Commissioned to paint a mural for the council hall in Florence's Palazzo Vecchio (remains unfinished).
  • 1515: Paints St. John the Baptist.
  • 1516: The king of France invites Da Vinci to come work for him.
  • 1519: Dies in France.
